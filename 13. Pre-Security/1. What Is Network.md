## Task 1: What is Networking?
<img width="2048" height="1536" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4c8ab72d-c84d-470b-aa1b-e68c20191b6e" />

A network is simply a group of things that are connected together.

In real life, your friends form a network because you’re connected by shared interests.
In technology, devices like phones and computers form networks to share information.

### Examples of Networks in Everyday Life

Networks are everywhere around us:
- Public transportation systems (buses, trains connected across a city)
- Power grids (electricity delivered to homes)
- Postal systems (sending letters and packages)
- Social connections (friends, neighbors)


### Networks in Computing

In computing, a network is when devices are connected to communicate.

Examples of devices in a network:
- Phones
- Laptops
- Security cameras
- Traffic lights
- Smart farming equipment

These devices send and receive data using rules (called protocols, though not detailed here).



### Size of Networks

Networks can be:

- ``Small`` → just 2 devices (like your phone and laptop)
- ``Huge`` → billions of devices (like the internet)

## Task 2: What is the Internet?
The Internet is like a huge network made up of many smaller networks.

Each small network (like Alice, Bob, Jim) is connected to others
Together, they form the Internet

In your example:

- Alice knows Bob and Jim
- Alice also knows Zayn and Toby
- But Bob and Jim can’t understand Zayn and Toby

So Alice acts as a messenger, passing messages between them

<img width="712" height="800" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4ac51dac-88d2-417c-92f4-0c001e78a8f2" />

- Alice = a device/router that can connect different groups
- Because she understands both sides, she helps them communicate

In computing, this is how the Internet works:

Different networks connect through systems that can translate and pass data

### Short History
- `1960s` – ARPANET: First real network, funded by the U.S. Defence Department
- `1989` – Tim Berners-Lee: Created the World Wide Web, making the Internet useful for sharing information
